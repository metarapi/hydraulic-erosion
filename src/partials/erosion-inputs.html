Droplets

Number of water droplets used to simulate terrain erosion.

Function

Determines how many individual droplets will traverse the terrain during each simulation cycle.

Impact

  • Higher Values: Increase the simulation's detail and accuracy by allowing more interactions with the terrain, but may require more computational resources and time.
  • Lower Values: Reduce computational load and speed up the simulation, but may result in less detailed erosion patterns.

Recommendation

Adjust based on desired simulation detail and available processing power.

Cycles

Number of simulation cycles to run.

Function

Specifies how many complete passes the erosion simulation will perform over the terrain.

Impact

  • Higher Values: Allow more extensive erosion and deposition processes, leading to more pronounced terrain changes.
  • Lower Values: Limit the extent of terrain modification, resulting in subtler changes.

Recommendation

Choose a value that balances the desired level of terrain transformation with the time required for the simulation.

Fixed Point Scale

Scaling factor to maintain precision in heightmap calculations.

Function

Multiplies height values to preserve decimal precision in integer-based computations.

Impact

  • Higher Scale: Increases precision, allowing for finer elevation changes, but may lead to larger memory usage.
  • Lower Scale: Reduces precision, potentially causing noticeable elevation steps or artifacts in the terrain.

Recommendation

Set a scale that offers a balance between computational precision and memory efficiency based on the simulation's requirements.

Erosion Rate

Rate at which droplets erode the terrain.

Function

Controls how much sediment a droplet removes from the terrain per interaction.

Impact

  • Higher Rates: Accelerate erosion, leading to quicker terrain degradation but may oversimplify realistic processes.
  • Lower Rates: Slow down erosion, resulting in more gradual and potentially more realistic terrain changes.

Recommendation

Adjust to simulate different erosion intensities; higher for aggressive erosion scenarios and lower for gentle landscapes.

Deposition Rate

Rate at which droplets deposit sediment onto the terrain.

Function

Determines how much sediment a droplet adds to the terrain per interaction.

Impact

  • Higher Rates: Increase sediment deposition, potentially creating more prominent geological features like dunes or deltas.
  • Lower Rates: Decrease deposition, resulting in less noticeable buildup and flatter terrain areas.

Recommendation

Use higher rates to model environments with abundant sediment or strong deposition forces, and lower rates for sparse sediment scenarios.

Barrier Interval

Frequency (in iterations) to synchronize simulation processes.

Function

Sets how often (in terms of simulation iterations) the system enforces memory and execution synchronization barriers.

Impact

  • Higher Intervals: Reduce the frequency of synchronization, potentially increasing performance but risking data consistency issues.
  • Lower Intervals: Ensure more frequent synchronization, enhancing data integrity at the cost of performance.

Recommendation

Balance synchronization frequency to maintain simulation accuracy without significantly hindering performance. Typically, values between 5-10 are effective.

Draw Interval

Frequency (in cycles) to redraw the eroded terrain.

Function

Sets after how many erosion cycles will the terrain be rendered.

Impact

  • Higher Intervals: Reduce the frequency of drawing, improving performance, especialy for larger heightmaps.
  • Lower Intervals: Increase the frequency of drawing, providing more detailed visualization of the erosion process.

Recommendation

For a heightmap size of 256, smaller values work well. For 1024 and larger, consider using higher intervals to balance performance and visualization detail.
